AEM — Corporate, Product & Patient Support Pages

1. Project Overview

After two senior members left the team, I took on leadership of a three‑person AEM project for a pharmaceutical client. I guided two new members, managed schedules, and maintained stable content operations while ensuring high‑quality Japanese localization.

2. Team Structure & Duration

Team size: 3 members (Member → Lead)
Duration: Oct 2017 – Oct 2020

3. Client Challenges & Goals

Frequent design updates and localization QA caused inconsistencies between global and Japanese versions. The client needed reliable delivery and quality control despite team changes.

4. Solutions & Contributions

  • Led two new members and managed project progress after senior departures
  • Standardized QA and publishing workflows to maintain stability
  • Coordinated design and schedule adjustments with Japanese stakeholders
  • Communicated with global teams in English to align specifications

5. Improvements & Focus Areas

  • Enhanced readability and layout stability for Japanese content
  • Proposed improvements to page structure and user flow
  • Reduced miscommunication by organizing and clarifying requirements

6. Results & KPI

  • Maintained on‑time releases despite team turnover
  • Received positive client feedback for leadership and quality
  • Recognized from the client for project management and mentoring
  • Received recognition for maintaining stable operations and leading the team after senior members left

Proprietary CMS — New Page Builds & Publishing

1. Project Overview

Began working with a newly introduced proprietary CMS from the ground up. Learned its framework independently, created sample pages to test functionality, and then built new localized pages for the Japanese market including home, landing, and web seminar pages.

2. Team Structure & Duration

Team size: 3 members
Duration: 2021 – 2023 (ongoing)

3. Client Challenges & Goals

The client needed rapid page creation and stable publishing in a CMS that was still under development. Japanese text required careful layout handling, and QA processes had to be standardized to maintain quality.

4. Solutions & Contributions

  • Explored and documented CMS functions through trial builds and internal testing
  • Developed new pages while ensuring consistent design and usability across devices
  • Created a repeatable QA checklist for Japanese content validation
  • Shared CMS usage tips and troubleshooting notes with team members

5. Improvements & Focus Areas

  • Improved workflow efficiency by defining clear publishing steps
  • Enhanced Japanese UX through layout and readability adjustments
  • Built team confidence in using the new CMS through documentation and mentoring

6. Results & KPI

  • Delivered new pages faster with fewer post‑release corrections
  • Maintained consistent quality across multiple site types
  • Received positive client feedback for initiative and reliability
